The problem I lived every day

Every morning, managing accounts for four business entities — Pro Synergy Medical Systems, Propharma Maldives, Synergy Care Clinic, and Medex Pharmacy — I watched the same scene play out. Staff would hand-write a payment voucher, attach a paper invoice, walk it to my desk, I would check it, sign it, then walk it to the MD's office. The MD would sign it, I would file it, and eventually make the bank transfer. The next morning, someone would call asking "where is my payment?" — and I would start hunting through a pile of paper.

I had been doing this for years. And across Male', across Colombo, across every small business in South Asia and beyond — so was everyone else. Paper payment vouchers, manual approval chains, lost invoices, and no audit trail. It was 2026, and the majority of SMEs were still running payments on paper.

"I didn't set out to build a software company. I set out to solve my own problem — and then realised it was everyone's problem."

Building with AI as a partner

What made PayFlow Pro possible was a new kind of development partnership: working with Claude, Anthropic's AI assistant, as a co-creator. I know accounts. I know IFRS. I know what a Head of Accounts needs at 9am when three urgent payments are sitting on her desk and the MD is in a meeting. But I don't have a computer science degree. Using Claude, I could describe exactly what I needed and have it built — feature by feature, screen by screen. The AI handled the code; I handled the product thinking.

This isn't just a story about one app. It's a story about what becomes possible when deep domain expertise meets AI capability. PayFlow Pro is the proof of concept — but the model applies everywhere. Any expert, in any field, can now build the tool they always wished existed.

What PayFlow Pro means to me

This is not a side project. This is the crystallisation of years of operational experience, accounting knowledge, and a genuine frustration with how things work. Every feature in PayFlow Pro — the cheque printing for Maldivian banks, the dual MVR/USD workflow, the Deloitte-audit-ready history trail, the QB Online sync — came from a real problem I personally experienced or saw colleagues struggle with. It is built for the accountant who has to explain to the MD why a payment is delayed. It is built for the staff member who doesn't know which ledger account to use. It is built for the business owner who wants to approve payments from their phone at 10pm. It is, simply, the system I always wished I had.
